A digital reconstruction of the dermal-epidermal
junction of the skin in three dimensions using
optical coherence tomography images

Responsible for developing a digital reconstruction of the dermal-epidermal junction (DEJ) of the skin via computer aided design (CAD) to form 3D models using Autodesk Fusion 360 and Inventor, a 3D modelling software. The 3D models were produced using additive manufacturing and laser-based patterning using SLA 3D printers.
I developed high resolution 3D models with sinusoidal waveform topography, specifically defined by cellular dimensions. In this process 3D printing paraments were optimised, grey resin was used for greater resolution and print orientation was explored. Additionally, I modelled cubic patterns in cellular dimension and a more accurate 3D model of the human DEJ created using a stack of 400 optical coherence tomography (OCT) images of the isolated DEJ to further assess the effectiveness of topography of each model on cells during cell culture.
The in-vivo OCT data was collected using a VivoSight OCT Scanner, from the cubital fossa. I used MATLAB to filter the datasets and extrapolate missing data to enable the formation of a continuous 3D body. Each dataset holds 400 chronologically stacked images, once inputted into the Amira software the epidermis, DEJ and dermis are isolated via segmentation. 3D printed models were assessed via bioimaging techniques including optical microscopy and scanning electron microscopy.
I presented my work at a number of showcases and conferences. In addition to this I am currently working on an upcoming publication titled "A digital reconstruction of the dermal-epidermal junction of the skin in three dimensions using optical coherence tomography images".

Electrochemically-Driven Assembly of Chitosan
Hydrogels on PEDOT for Bioelectrodes

I conducted experiments to develop Interpenetrating Network of PEDOT/Chitosan Conductive Soft Hydrogel for Bioelectronic Applications. This involves a two-step procedure via potentiostatic electrodeposition.
In a solution containing individual 3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene monomers, sodium dodecyl sulfate surfactant and tripolyphosphate, a gold electrode is added. A potential was applied to promote the joining of monomers; electropolymerization. This coated gold electrode is added to a viscous solution containing sodium chloride, acetic acid and chitosan. A voltage was applied to promote hydrogel assembly. The second step aims to create an interpenetrating network between the PEDOT and Chitosan matrices. On completion of each stage the electrical properties of the hybrid hydrogel were assessed via cyclic voltammetry and electrochemical impedance spectroscopy.
As a result of my work I was a SURE 2022 Student Researcher of the Year Nominee.

ScARRzio: Scar-Tissue Rehabilitation Bot

Scar trauma due to injury, surgery or burns that has lasting physical and psychological impacts on patients. This includes limited scar tissue (ST) mobility, itching, sensitivity, pain, and uneven collagen formation which can cause considerable pain, discomfort and mental health issues. ST massage is known to help reduce the severity of physical symptoms, especially when treatment is followed consistently [1]. Nonetheless, patients often cut treatment short due to factors like cost (£5-30 per visit), fewer appointments and long waiting times, or being part of an at-risk group. High-quality, biomechatronic physiotherapy offers two key benefits: easily accessible therapy for patients and freeing time for physiotherapists (PT) to focus on complex and time-sensitive care. While basic massaging devices exist, low degrees-of-freedom (d.f.) and personalization make them a poor substitute for in-person physiotherapy. Thus, we propose a high d.f., portable, closed-loop massaging device that mimics a human PT.
